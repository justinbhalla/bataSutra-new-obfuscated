Your Body Clock Isn’t One Clock — It’s Many (and They Fight at Night)

You were told you have “a circadian rhythm.” Reality is stranger and more human: your brain, gut, liver, hormones, and immune system run separate clocks — sometimes beautifully aligned, sometimes quietly arguing with each other.
By bataSutra Editorial · December 20th 2025

The short

  • Not one clock: Your brain clock keeps time; your body clocks follow — but only if life cooperates.
  • When they drift: you feel “off” — foggy, wired but tired, hungry at strange hours, emotionally thin.
  • Why now: late nights, screens, irregular meals, travel, shift work — modern life splits clocks apart.
  • Science shift: newer research shows multiple internal clocks negotiate daily balance.
  • Reality: tiredness is often biology misaligned, not weakness.

Your internal “parliament” of clocks

There isn’t one master rhythm. There’s a committee:

SystemWhat its clock controls
BrainAlertness, mood readiness, timing of sleep pressure
GutDigestion readiness, gut motility, hunger signaling
Liver / MetabolismGlucose handling, energy readiness
Immune SystemInflammation windows, repair cycles

On good days, they move like an orchestra. On tougher days, one section accelerates while another drifts behind. That mismatch is what “feeling off” actually feels like.

What happens when clocks fight

Misalignment feels familiar:

  • You wake up but your focus doesn’t.
  • You eat but don’t really feel satisfied.
  • You feel tired but can’t fall asleep.
  • Your emotions flare faster than usual.

This isn’t personality failure. It’s rhythm failure. And it happens far more than people admit.

The modern world breaks rhythm on purpose

Screens delay sleep signals. Late eating confuses gut timing. Travel shreds internal coordination. Shift work bends everything against biology.

The result? Millions walk around synchronized externally (the clock says “day”), but internally out of tune.

The big emotional truth

When you say:
“I don’t feel like myself lately.”

Often what you mean is:
“My clocks stopped agreeing.”

That realization replaces guilt with understanding — and understanding is powerful.

What to watch

Watch consistency. Regular light, eating, movement, and sleep create alignment.
