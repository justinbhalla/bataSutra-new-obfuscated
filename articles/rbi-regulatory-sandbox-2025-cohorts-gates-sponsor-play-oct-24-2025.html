The short

  • Status: Theme-neutral, on-tap window is open (since Apr 2025). Expect 6–9 months end-to-end for most pilots.
  • Fit: Tests needing real customers under tight guardrails—UPI offline edges, underwriting with capped limits, RegTech controls.
  • Tripwires: Auto-pause if complaints > 0.3% of sample or NCL > guardrail; deletion proof mandatory at exit.
  • Bank capacity: Sponsor SLAs (onboarding/day caps, dispute TAT) are the bottleneck—lock these before Gate-2.
  • Production: Graduation permits only the tested scope; any expansion is a new approval conversation.

Sponsor-bank models & SLAs

Models

  • Embedded sponsor: Bank runs rails; fintech supplies UX/risk.
  • Aggregator hub: Multi-bank integration with common SLA.
  • Direct-bank pilot: One bank; shortest approvals; capacity-limited.

SLA guardrails

  • Daily onboarding caps; dispute TAT; fraud ops staffing.
  • Data-sharing contract: fields, purpose, retention, deletion, breach window.
  • Change management: versioning; rollback; stakeholder notice.

Compliance & audit checklist

  • MVD plan: Event schema, PII map, retention/deletion, encryption.
  • Consent artifacts: Screenshots/timestamps; withdrawal path.
  • Incident playbook: Thresholds, comms templates, regulator flow.
  • Sandbox logs: Immutable run-logs; access audits; drift notes.
  • Post-test deletion: Certificates from all (sub)processors.

FAQ

  • Is on-tap indefinite? It’s open, but capacity and themes steer prioritisation.
  • How big can a pilot be? Start small; expand only after KPI proof within risk caps.
  • Does graduation equal blanket approval? No—only within tested scope and safeguards.
