Data-Center Cool: Air vs Liquid — Where the Break-Even Sits

AI racks over ~30 kW tilt the math: liquid cuts cooling energy and floor space, air hits noise and hot-aisle limits.
By bataSutra Editorial · November 1, 2025

The short

  • Threshold: Above ~30 kW per rack, air’s cost and complexity ramp fast.
  • Shift: Operators pilot direct-to-chip as AI loads push power density up.
  • Today: Adoption still gradual, but pressure is building as new GPUs land.

Break-even grid (energy OPEX focus)

Assumptions: average power price $0.10/kWh; IT load 24×7; air-cooling overhead ~25% of IT power at 20 kW, rising toward ~40% at 50 kW; liquid cuts that overhead ~15–30% depending on design.

Why the tilt is accelerating

  • Next-gen GPU trays push rack densities beyond what air paths can handle sustainably.
  • Floor-space relief: higher kW per rack with liquid means fewer racks for the same compute.
  • Noise/thermal comfort: air paths at high kW raise fan energy and acoustic issues.

What to watch

  • Operator surveys show liquid interest rising as densities creep up; expect more 2026 pilots to convert to standard builds.
  • Vendor roadmaps: direct-to-chip kits aligned with new GPU platforms; immersion where water risk is a concern.
  • Facility upgrades: secondary loops, CDU placement, and pad-level heat rejection optimized for warm-water designs.
Notes: Adoption trajectory and density thresholds based on industry surveys and vendor guidance through 2025; OPEX grid uses stated assumptions and will vary by climate, PUE, water temps, and tariff.
