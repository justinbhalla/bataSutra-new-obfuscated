Global Grocery Chains: Private Label’s Quiet Takeover

Europe hits record share, the U.K. crosses half the basket, and the U.S. grind higher continues—why shoppers aren’t switching back.
By bataSutra Editorial · November 1, 2025

The short

  • Europe: Store brands at ~38% of value sales—new high.
  • U.K.: Own-label now above 50% of grocery sales.
  • U.S.: Dollar share ~21% and rising as price gaps stay wide.
  • Why it sticks: Quality parity in staples + premium tiers in beauty/pet care.

Category standouts (global): dairy, household paper, ambient staples; premium growth in beauty/pet care as retailers push top tiers.

Price gap & premium creep

Even as inflation cools, the shelf delta often stays in the 25–30% range versus national brands. Retailers are reinvesting a slice of that into better packaging, cleaner labels, and premium store sub-brands—enough to lock in new habits. Expect more beauty and pet care entries where margins can support R&D and branding without breaking the “value” narrative.

What to watch next

  • Beauty & pet care: rising private lines at big-box and hard discounters.
  • Fresh & chilled: continued push where trust and quality signals matter.
  • U.S. holiday season: whether promo calendars narrow or widen the price gap.
