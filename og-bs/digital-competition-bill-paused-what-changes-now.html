Digital Competition Bill Paused: What Changes Now for Big Tech & Startups

The ex-ante regime is on hold while thresholds and scope are re-evaluated. Here’s the practical impact, what still applies under CCI, and how to prep for what’s next.
By bataSutra Editorial · August 20, 2025
In this piece:
  • What’s paused vs. what continues under current law
  • How a phased/targeted approach could look
  • Actionables for platforms, app developers, and startups
  • FAQ on timelines, thresholds, and interim enforcement

The short

  • Pause, not scrap: The Digital Competition Bill (ex-ante) is paused for re-assessment of thresholds and startup impact. No firm timeline.
  • Ex-post stays: The Competition Act and CCI’s case-by-case enforcement continue—self-preferencing, tying, and exclusivity can still be investigated.
  • Phased path likely: A parliamentary panel has favoured phased adoption; government may refine scope, do market studies, or pilot sector-specific rules.

What’s on pause

  • Ex-ante obligations for “gatekeeper” platforms (a.k.a. SSDEs): pre-defined do’s/don’ts around app stores, default placements, interoperability, and data mixing.
  • Bright-line thresholds to designate SSDEs (turnover/users/market-cap metrics) are being reconsidered.
  • Timelines for compliance, audits, and penalties under a new standalone law.

What still applies today

AreaWhat’s in forceWhat it means
Competition law CCI’s ex-post enforcement under the Competition Act. Conduct (not status) led: cases on self-preferencing, predatory pricing, exclusive dealing can proceed.
Sector rules RBI, MeitY, TRAI, DoCA, etc. continue their sectoral mandates. Data, payments, telecom, consumer-protection norms still bite.
App store billing Ongoing litigation/regulatory directions continue to shape billing & anti-steering outcomes. Contract/UX changes may be required via orders/settlements rather than a new statute.

What could replace a one-shot DCB

  • Phased adoption: Start with a few high-risk practices; expand after review periods.
  • Targeted thresholds: Define “systemically significant” by Indian market impact, not just global size; allow sunset/appeal on designation.
  • Market studies first: Run focused studies (ads, app distribution, search, e-commerce logistics) before hard rules.
  • Inter-regulator alignment: Coordinate with sector regulators to avoid overlap and forum shopping.

Actionables

For Big Tech/platforms

  • Review self-preferencing, ranking, and default settings; document objective criteria.
  • Enable fair access (APIs/data portability) where feasible; log refusal rationales.
  • Map data-combining practices; add opt-outs and clear disclosures.

For startups/app developers

  • Track billing and anti-steering updates per platform; keep alt-payment UX ready.
  • Preserve evidence of discrimination (screens, logs, contracts) for quick filings if needed.
  • Design for interoperability (data export/import) to benefit if portability rules arrive.

FAQ

  • Is the bill dead? No—paused. Government is reassessing scope and thresholds; a phased or sectoral approach could return.
  • What’s the timeline? None announced. Watch MCA/Parliamentary updates and CCI case dockets.
  • Should startups wait? No. Continue compliance under existing law; prepare playbooks for billing, data portability, and discovery neutrality.
