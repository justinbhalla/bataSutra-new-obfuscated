bataSutra | June 4, 2025

Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) in India are facing a surge of sophisticated fraud. The latest threat? Deepfakes. These AI-generated fake videos are being used to trick video KYC systems during the loan approval process, enabling fraudsters to secure loans using stolen identities.

How it works

Fraudsters scrape public videos and photos of individuals, then use AI tools to generate fake video streams that pass as legitimate customers. These deepfakes often bypass video-based KYC checks implemented by NBFCs under regulatory mandates.

Vulnerabilities in digital KYC

While video KYC helped scale financial inclusion during COVID-era digitization, its reliance on visual confirmation makes it vulnerable to spoofing. Most NBFCs use facial matching but lack liveness detection or motion cues to detect deepfakes in real time.

Regulatory and tech responses

Experts expect the RBI and other regulatory bodies to recommend stronger protocols â€” including biometric checks, two-factor authentication, and AI-based fraud scoring. Some firms are exploring blockchain-based KYC systems for tamper-proof verification trails.

The bata takeaway

India's fintech backbone is under siege by generative AI. As NBFCs digitize further, fraud will evolve â€” and so must defense. Institutions must urgently update their KYC stack to avoid becoming easy prey.
