India’s Green Hydrogen Bet: Hype, Hope or Hard Reality?

bataSutra | August 01, 2025

With a ₹19,000 crore commitment and ambitions to become a global hydrogen hub, India’s big green fuel dream is finally taking shape — but is it grounded in reality?

The Promise

Green hydrogen, produced via electrolysis using renewable energy, is touted as the fuel of the future. It can decarbonize industries like steel, fertilizer, and transport — all major emitters in India’s climate equation.

The Players

NTPC, Reliance, and Adani are betting big, with pilot plants already announced in Gujarat, Ladakh, and Tamil Nadu. Meanwhile, startups are innovating around low-cost electrolyzers and storage tech.

Reality Check

Production costs are still high, grid integration is patchy, and India’s electrolyser manufacturing capacity is nascent. Experts warn: green hydrogen is not a 2025 solution — it’s a 2035 goal.

The bata takeaway

Green hydrogen is no silver bullet, but it could be a green arrow — pointing India towards cleaner growth. The challenge is to stay on target without burning out early.
