Where breadth and relative strength are clustering now — and how to position without chasing euphoria.

The short

  • Leadership: Banks and capital goods retain RS on earnings visibility and backlog execution.
  • Improving: Autos, pharma, power add breadth; watch dealer inventory and US pricing pressure.
  • Cooling: IT midcaps on uneven ramps; stick to cost-takeout/automation leaders with TCV proof.
  • Froth: Low-float microcaps with weak cash conversion—avoid illiquid spikes near events.

Story prompts: “PSU bank leadership & deposit beta,” “Capex flywheel—orders vs execution.”

Sector drivers: what’s fueling moves

Banks & Financials

Steady loan growth and benign asset quality keep multiples supported. Watch: deposit beta creep; wholesale funding share.

Capital Goods/Industrials

High backlogs and improving execution cadence; margins hinge on mix and input costs. Watch: order-to-revenue conversion.

Autos

Festive builds and model refreshes support PVs; 2W recovery uneven. Watch: dealer weeks, discounting.

Pharma

US launches plus resilient India biz; remediation progress in focus. Watch: US price erosion vs mix.

IT Services

Lumpy ramps; budgets guarded. Watch: large-deal TCV, onsite mix, margin defense.

Power/Utilities

Demand strength + tariff clarity; receivables discipline improving. Watch: PLFs, fuel linkages.

Breadth & revision gauges

  • Advance/Decline: Add risk if >1.4× alongside green leaders; cut beta if <0.9×.
  • Revisions (net): Track upgrades–downgrades by sector; turn cautious if leaders flip negative.
  • Smallcap/Nifty ratio: Fade extremes; avoid illiquid adds near prior ratio highs.

Risk checks & exits

  • Trim if a leader underperforms Nifty by 5–7% over 4 weeks or net revisions turn negative.
  • Respect liquidity bands; size by ADTV × 10 for small/microcaps.
  • Two failed signals in a session → stand down; protect weekly P&L.
