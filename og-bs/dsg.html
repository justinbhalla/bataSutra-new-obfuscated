Desi SaaS, Global Class: How India is Quietly Owning the Software Stack

bataSutra | August 01, 2025

India’s software-as-a-service (SaaS) exports have crossed $30B — but unlike IT services, this isn’t about cheap talent. It’s about deep product, built for global pain points.

The Small-Town Software Surge

Companies like Zoho (Tenkasi), Chargebee (Chennai), and Postman (Bangalore) are hiring from Tier-2 cities, tapping into sharp but under-the-radar talent to scale product and support globally.

Winning with Frugality

Indian SaaS startups are mastering lean go-to-market (GTM) strategies: outbound email, inside sales, freemium models. No flashy conferences, just functional funnels.

Investor Confidence, Global Traction

VCs from Sequoia to Tiger Global are doubling down, not just on unicorns but on sub-$10M ARR startups showing global logos and retention metrics. The playbook: build in India, bill in USD.

The bata takeaway

SaaS may be India’s next IT — but this time, it’s product-led, design-driven, and dollar-priced. Quiet execution is now loud success.
