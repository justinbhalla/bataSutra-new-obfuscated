Inside India’s EV2.0 Moment: Batteries, Beyond the Buzz

bataSutra | August 01, 2025

India’s first wave of electric mobility was all about scooters and subsidies. EV 2.0 is about something far more critical: batteries, IP, and supply chain control.

From Assembling to Innovating

Startups like Log9 Materials, Exponent Energy, and Gegadyne are no longer just integrating off-the-shelf batteries — they’re building fast-charging chemistries and India-friendly thermal systems.

Localizing the Supply Chain

With lithium sourcing still tricky, focus is shifting to battery recycling and alternative chemistries like sodium-ion and aluminum-air. Tamil Nadu and Telangana are emerging as battery manufacturing zones.

The Policy Push

The government’s PLI scheme and battery swapping frameworks are encouraging deeper localization. But the real gamechanger? R&D tax credits that reward innovation over assembly.

The bata takeaway

India’s EV story is no longer about who sells the most scooters — it’s about who owns the core tech. And in EV 2.0, batteries aren’t just parts. They’re power.
