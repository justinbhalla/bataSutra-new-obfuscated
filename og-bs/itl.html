From Loans to Livelihoods: How Embedded Finance is Powering India’s Kirana Stores

bataSutra | August 01, 2025

India’s kirana stores aren’t just stocking Maggi and milk anymore — they’re becoming gateways to credit, insurance, and payments, thanks to a silent fintech revolution.

The Quiet Financial Infiltration

With over 13 million stores across the country, kiranas are being onboarded by startups offering embedded finance — seamlessly integrated loans, BNPL, and savings products delivered right through POS systems and apps.

Players Behind the Curtain

Fintechs like Jumbotail, Kaleidofin, and PayNearby are tapping into transaction data to assess creditworthiness. Result: shopkeepers can access working capital within 24 hours — no collateral, no paperwork.

Trust, Tech, and Turnover

With rising digital literacy, even semi-urban and rural shopkeepers are adopting tools that track sales, enable QR payments, and offer micro-insurance. Many are becoming local “mini banks” — especially for underbanked communities.

The bata takeaway

Kirana stores have always been the economic spine of Bharat. Now, they’re evolving into smart financial hubs — one QR scan at a time.
