Disclosure focus — unit economics & governance

Unit economics

  • Cohort LTV/CAC with payback months (M1, M3, M6, M12 cadence).
  • Effective take-rate (after incentives) by category; contribution margin (CM2) ladder.
  • OCF/EBITDA trajectory and reconciliation to audited notes.

Governance

  • Related-party transactions with pricing/tenure clarity.
  • Contingent liabilities, guarantees, and promoter pledges.
  • ESOP overhang and dilution math across scenarios.

FPI hygiene — what’s changing on the ground

  • Beneficial ownership clarity: Look-through structures expected; KYC depth up front reduces back-and-forth.
  • Segregation: Pooled vehicles mapped to avoid “acting in concert” optics at allocation and lock-in.
  • Flow stability: Cleaner registers improve anchor quality and post-listing liquidity texture.

Playbooks

Issuers

  • Publish a one-pager metrics glossary; lock definitions early.
  • Stand up cohort/CM2 dashboards that reconcile to audited numbers.
  • Run mock Q&A with bankers/legal before DRHP/RHP.

Bankers

  • Pre-flight governance (RPTs, pledges, vendor dependencies).
  • Anchor mapping by mandate type (growth vs yield) and lock-in preference.
  • Educate on valuation bridges (private → public medians) with sensitivity ranges.

Investors

  • Focus on cash conversion (OCF/EBITDA) and cohort durability over GMV optics.
  • Size bids by free float and anchor dispersion; beware of bunched exits post lock-in.
  • Use scenario tables: CM2 ± take-rate, CAC normalization, claim curves.

FAQ

  • Does faster mean riskier? No—speed targets process friction, not disclosure depth.
  • What if metrics are early-stage? Show trajectory, sensitivity, and reconciliation; be explicit about assumptions.
