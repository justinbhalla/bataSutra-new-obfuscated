Skye Is Not the Limit: India’s Drone Grid Lifts Off

bataSutra | June 16, 2025

Skye Air is quietly solving one of India’s biggest medical logistics challenges — by looking up. Its autonomous drone network now connects 40+ remote clinics and labs in Uttarakhand, reducing medicine delivery times from 5 hours to 35 minutes.

What’s Different This Time?

Unlike earlier drone hype cycles, Skye Air’s approach is fully integrated with state health systems. Their dashboard offers real-time temperature monitoring, trip logs, and BVLOS compliance — a crucial feature for high-value payloads.

From Trial to Infrastructure

With funding from SIDBI and support from the Make in India drone PLI scheme, Skye Air is going from demo flights to operational routes. Their vision? A national drone corridor network serving both public health and pharma ecommerce.

The Numbers So Far

Since January 2025:

  • 520 successful flights
  • 1000+ km of route coverage
  • Avg delivery speed: 40km/hr

Next Hubs: Assam, Nagaland, and Chhattisgarh

Discussions are underway with state governments to deploy drone logistics where terrain and infrastructure are limiting factors. The Ministry of Civil Aviation is also mapping routes for a “National Drone Supergrid.”

The bata takeaway

This isn’t just tech for show. Skye Air is laying the groundwork for a logistics revolution that flies over legacy infrastructure — one urgent delivery at a time.
