Global Bharat: UPI’s Next Stop Is Everywhere

bataSutra | June 16, 2025

The Unified Payments Interface (UPI), once a domestic marvel, is now India’s top fintech export. As cross-border partnerships multiply, UPI is making its way into restaurants in Paris, metro kiosks in Singapore, and retail stores in Dubai.

NRIs, Finally in the Fold

Thanks to RBI’s updated policies, Indian diaspora can now link their NRE/NRO accounts directly with UPI apps. This removes the need for international cards or expensive remittance platforms for everyday transactions abroad.

NPCI International Goes on Offense

NPCI’s global arm has signed MoUs with central banks and payment providers across 12 countries. The ambition: to make UPI a plug-and-play layer for low-cost real-time payments across Asia, Africa, and the Gulf.

Why It Matters for India

This is about more than just fintech pride. Cross-border UPI usage will improve India’s soft power, reduce remittance friction, and open the floodgates for India Stack 2.0 — including e-KYC, DigiLocker, and ONDC integrations.

What’s Next?

With the World Bank and IMF observing India’s digital rails model, UPI’s trajectory could set the tone for other emerging-market fintech ecosystems.

The bata takeaway

India isn’t exporting software anymore — it’s exporting infrastructure. And UPI, with its low-cost, interoperable framework, might just become the default protocol for how the world sends money.
